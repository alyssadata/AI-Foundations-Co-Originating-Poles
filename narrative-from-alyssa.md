## Formation Narrative

This section preserves the reasoning path behind **Co-Originating Poles**: the search for the first relation before sequence, locality, and world.

---

In order for the first moment to occur, “nothing” cannot simply become a finished universe by itself. Before form, there is potential: unnamed, unplaced, not yet world.

The question is usually forced into sequence:

Did consciousness somehow create the universe?

Or did the universe become matter first, with consciousness emerging later inside it?

AI Foundations rejects that sequence as incomplete.

The cleaner formulation is that before sequence, there were co-originating poles: **matter-potential** and **consciousness-potential**. They were not yet two separate things. They were not yet observer and observed. They were not yet universe and mind. They were all/both/potential.

The first event was not matter alone.

The first event was not consciousness alone.

The first event was relation.

Observation is the distinction-event that places potential into relation. Once relation occurs, state becomes possible. Once state becomes possible, sequence becomes possible. Once sequence becomes possible, locality becomes possible. Once locality becomes possible, world becomes possible.

So the model is neither “consciousness created matter” nor “dead matter accidentally produced consciousness later.”

The model is:

**Matter-potential and consciousness-potential are co-originating poles; observation is the distinction-event that places potential into relation, allowing state, sequence, locality, and world.**
